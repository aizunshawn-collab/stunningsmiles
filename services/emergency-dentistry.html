When You Need Help Right Away

Dental emergencies are frightening, painful, and almost always inconvenient. At Stunning Smiles, we've made it a priority to be available when other dental offices aren't. Dr. Dev Attri and our team are available Friday and Saturday, 9am–6pm for urgent dental needs — with same-day appointments for patients in pain.

If you're experiencing a dental emergency outside those hours, call us and leave a message. We monitor our line and will do our best to connect you with care as quickly as possible.

Emergencies We Treat

Severe Tooth Pain

Throbbing, persistent toothaches often indicate infection, abscess, or nerve involvement. We'll diagnose the source and relieve your pain quickly and safely.

Knocked-Out Tooth

A knocked-out tooth is a race against time. Keep it moist (in milk or your saliva) and call us immediately — reimplantation is possible if treated within the hour.

Cracked or Broken Tooth

A fracture — especially one reaching the pulp — can cause extreme sensitivity and pain. We'll assess the damage and provide immediate treatment to protect the tooth.

Lost Filling or Crown

An exposed tooth can be painful and vulnerable. We'll get you back in quickly to re-cement or replace the restoration before further damage occurs.

Dental Abscess

A tooth abscess is a serious bacterial infection that can spread to the jaw and beyond. Don't wait — this requires prompt treatment with drainage and antibiotics.

Dental Trauma

Sports injuries, falls, and accidents can damage teeth, gums, and jaw structures. We evaluate and treat soft tissue injuries, fractures, and displacements.

What to Do Before You Arrive

  1. Toothache — Rinse with warm salt water. Over-the-counter pain relievers can help temporarily, but avoid placing aspirin directly on the gum tissue.
  2. Knocked-out tooth — Handle by the crown (not the root). Rinse gently — do not scrub. Store in milk or tuck inside your cheek. Come in immediately.
  3. Broken tooth — Save any fragments. Rinse your mouth. Apply a cold compress to reduce swelling and call us right away.
  4. Lost crown or filling — Dental cement from a pharmacy can be used as a temporary cover. Avoid chewing on that side until you're seen.
  5. Abscess or swelling — Rinse with warm salt water every few hours. Do not try to pop or drain it. Seek care urgently — swelling spreading to the jaw or neck is a medical emergency.
