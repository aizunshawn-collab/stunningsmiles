What Is Sleep Apnea?

Sleep apnea is a condition where your airway repeatedly collapses or becomes blocked during sleep, causing you to stop breathing for seconds at a time — sometimes hundreds of times a night. The most common form, obstructive sleep apnea (OSA), is caused by the soft tissues in the throat relaxing and blocking the airway.

Left untreated, sleep apnea significantly raises your risk of high blood pressure, heart disease, stroke, type 2 diabetes, and depression. It also devastates sleep quality — leaving you exhausted, irritable, and unable to concentrate even after a full night in bed.

Signs You May Have Sleep Apnea

Loud Snoring

Chronic, loud snoring — especially followed by gasping or choking sounds — is one of the most common signs of obstructive sleep apnea.

Daytime Fatigue

Waking up unrefreshed and feeling exhausted throughout the day despite seemingly adequate sleep hours is a hallmark symptom.

Morning Headaches

Frequent headaches upon waking, often caused by drops in blood oxygen levels during the night, are strongly associated with sleep apnea.

Poor Concentration

Difficulty focusing, memory problems, and mood changes are common in people whose sleep is constantly disrupted by apnea events.

Our Dental Solution: Oral Appliance Therapy

Many patients cannot tolerate CPAP machines — they're bulky, noisy, and uncomfortable. We offer a highly effective alternative: custom oral appliances that gently reposition your jaw while you sleep, keeping the airway open naturally.

  1. Screening & evaluation — We assess your symptoms and oral anatomy and may coordinate with your physician for a sleep study if needed.
  2. Custom fitting — Digital impressions are taken to create a device that fits your mouth precisely — no generic one-size solutions.
  3. Trial & adjustment — We fine-tune the appliance until you're comfortable and your symptoms are resolved.
  4. Ongoing monitoring — Regular check-ins ensure the appliance continues to work effectively and fits properly as your anatomy changes over time.
