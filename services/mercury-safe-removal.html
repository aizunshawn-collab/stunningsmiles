Mercury-Safe Amalgam Removal

The safest, most responsible way to remove silver amalgam fillings — protecting you, our team, and the environment.

Why Mercury Matters in Dentistry

Silver amalgam fillings contain approximately 50% elemental mercury. While regulatory bodies have maintained that amalgam is safe for most people, a growing body of research indicates that mercury vapor released from fillings — especially during chewing, grinding, and removal — can accumulate in body tissues over time.

As an IAOMT (International Academy of Oral Medicine and Toxicology) member, Dr. Dev Attri is trained and certified in the SMART protocol — the world's most comprehensive set of safety measures for amalgam removal, protecting everyone in the room and the environment outside of it.

The SMART Protocol: What We Do to Protect You

High-Volume Evacuation

Specialized suction equipment that captures mercury vapor and particles at the source, preventing inhalation during removal.

Rubber Dam & Isolation

A protective barrier is placed in your mouth to prevent amalgam particles from being swallowed. Copious water cooling minimizes mercury vapor release.

Sectioning Technique

Instead of grinding out old fillings, we section them into chunks — dramatically reducing the surface area releasing vapor and particles.

Responsible Disposal

All amalgam waste is collected by a certified amalgam separator and disposed of by a licensed hazardous waste company — never flushed into the water system.

The Removal Process

  1. Pre-appointment preparation — We may recommend certain supplements (vitamin C, chlorella) to support your body's detoxification in the days before removal.
  2. Full safety setup — Air purifiers, nitrile gloves, specialized suction, and rubber dam isolation are all placed before we begin.
  3. Sectioned removal — The amalgam is cut into large chunks and removed with minimal drilling and water cooling at all times.
  4. Tooth-colored replacement — The space is cleaned, prepared, and restored with a biocompatible composite resin or ceramic filling.
  5. Post-removal guidance — We provide aftercare instructions and supplement recommendations to support your recovery.
